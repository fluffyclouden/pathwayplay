Key Concepts in Biochemical Pathways

Regulation by Insulin and Glucagon: Insulin promotes energy storage, uptake of glucose into tissues, and anabolic pathways. Glucagon activates energy breakdown, release of glucose into the blood, and catabolic pathways.

Anabolism and Catabolism: Anabolism builds molecules (e.g., fatty acid synthesis), while catabolism breaks them down (e.g., beta oxidation).

ATP/ADP ratio and NADH/NAD+ ratio: High ATP/ADP and NADH/NAD+ ratios signal high energy and inhibit energy-producing pathways. The opposite is true for energy-utilizing pathways, which are activated by high ATP/ADP and NADH/NAD+ ratios, and inhibited by low energy and NADH.

Enzyme Specificity: Dehydrogenases involve NAD+/NADH, NADP+/NADPH, FAD/FADH₂; kinases transfer phosphate groups; mutases rearrange functional groups. Carboxylases usually require biotin/vitamin B7 as a cofactor, and dehydrogenases usually require TPP/vitamin B1.

Irreversible Steps: Key regulatory points of pathways are irreversible steps, such as PFK-1 in glycolysis or pyruvate carboxylase in gluconeogenesis.

Compartmentalization: Pathways are compartmentalized for efficient regulation, and to stop pathways from "bumping into each other" (e.g., glycolysis in the cytosol vs. Krebs in the mitochondria; fatty acid synthesis in the cytosol vs. beta-oxidation in the mitochondria).
